About the Experimental Forest

Kyoto Prefectural University has six "University Forests" in Kyoto Prefecture (Ohno, Ohe, Takagamine, Kuta, Hiyoshi, and Umegahata). Each forest has its own unique characteristics, including artificial forests of Japanese cedar and cypress, and natural forests such as beech. Each forest utilizes its unique features to host field training for students of the Department of Forest Science and conduct research by students and faculty. They are also popular with the prefecture's residents through public lectures such as the "University Forest Outdoor Seminar." Universities with forest science (or forestry) departments have established educational and research forests (University Forests) as affiliated facilities, serving as a venue for forest science education and research. At the same time, to ensure sustainable forest management, the University Forests are maintained according to management plans, including thinning and sales of standing trees, new plantings, supplementary plantings, undergrowth clearing, and pruning. Kyoto Prefectural University's experimental forests include the Oeda and Takagamine Experimental Forests, which were established when the university was still the Kyoto Prefectural Agricultural and Forestry School, the Ohno Experimental Forest, which was acquired when the university was promoted to the Kyoto Prefectural College of Agriculture and Forestry, and the Kuta Experimental Forest, which was established in 1983 (Showa 58), the Hiyoshi Experimental Forest in 1986 (Showa 61), and the Umegahata Experimental Forest in 2008 (Heisei 20), for a total area of ​​710.39 hectares.
